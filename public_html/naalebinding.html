About nålebinding

An ancient craft.

People have been making textils with nålebinding since the Stone Age (I believe), anyway I am certain that used it to make clothing in the Viking, Bronze, and Iron Ages here in Denmark. Before anybody knew how to knit or crochet, there were people nålebinding!

"Broken threads" are used when nålebinding, in contrast to the continuous thread in knitting or crochet. This is necessary since the work is done with a large needle with a small eye. The best result is obtained by felting or spit-joining the ends, as knots tend to show in the work.

"Ya gotta spit join!"

What I have made

I have made several scarfs, a hat, mittens, slippers and several purses that I have fulled or felted after making them with nålebinding. I really enjoy making stuff with this ancient craft--there aren't many patterns that you HAVE to follow, you can just do your own thing!
