About soap

I started making soap when I was down and out due to my back. I couldn't sit and spin or make lace or do any gardening or anything!

But I was able to make "glycerin melt" soap. I had some molds and bought some glycerine soap base, scents, colorings, and fancy oils. Making soap this way is quick and easy, since you just cut up the base soap, melt it in the microwave, mix it with whatever you want, and pour it into the molds!

I have since regained my health and have made "real" soap from lye and lard (kaustisk soda og svinefedt). I got the formula from a book called, "The Perfect Pumpkin".

"It's gotta make suds!"

What I have made

I have made several batches of soap from the glycerin base. I have made three batches with the pumpkin puree, and various oils and scents. I was really thrilled to make soap that could work up the suds (the first batch wasn't so good). I have yet to try a batch with crushed pumpkin seeds, and a batch with milk.
